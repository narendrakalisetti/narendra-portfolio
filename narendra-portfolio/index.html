MSc Dissertation

Applied deep learning research with direct relevance to UK financial fraud detection and media integrity.

MSc Applied Data Science · Teesside University · 2025–2026
Deepfake Detection Using Multi-Task Learning & Clustering

The problem: Deepfake media poses serious risks in UK financial services (identity fraud, account takeover) and law enforcement. Single-task models fail on unseen manipulation types — they can't generalise across the full attack surface.

Approach: Multi-Task Learning trains a single ResNet-50 backbone across multiple detection tasks simultaneously — sharing feature representations for better generalisation. Gender classification auxiliary task improves feature learning. Contrastive learning generates structured embeddings. K-Means classifies real vs fake without requiring labels for every sample.

Explainability: SHAP and LIME layers provide interpretable outputs — critical for regulatory acceptance in financial services and law enforcement.
